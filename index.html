National Longitudinal Study of 1972
The National Longitudinal Study of the High School Class of 1972 (NLS-72) was the first secondary longitudinal study conducted by NCES. NLS-72 collected data from 1972 high school seniors until 1986.

High School and Beyond
The second longitudinal study in the program was the High School and Beyond (HS&B) study, a dual-cohort study which surveyed the sophomore and senior classes of 1980.

National Education Longitudinal Study of 1988
The National Education Longitudinal Study of 1988 (NELS:88) began with a cohort of 8th-graders, following them until 2000, and collecting data on secondary and post-secondary experiences. Learn more about NELS:88

Educational Longitudinal Study of 2002
The Education Longitudinal Study of 2002 (ELS:2002), currently collecting data on students’ postsecondary outcomes, began with sophomores in 2002 and added a senior cohort in 2004.

High School Longitudinal Study of 2009
The most recent cohort in the program, the High School Longitudinal Study of 2009 (HSLS:09), has a STEM focus. HSLS:09 is currently collecting data on students’ secondary experiences.

Welcome to the
Department of Education’s
ED Data Inventory

We hope that you will find the information included in the inventory useful in your search for data about education.

WHAT: The ED Data Inventory includes data reported to the Department of Education as part of grant activities, along with administrative and statistical data assembled and maintained by the Department. The ED Data Inventory includes descriptive data about each data collection along with information on the specific data elements in each collection.

WHEN: In September 2010, the Department of Education formed a Data Strategy Team to improve the coordination of data collections across program offices. The Data Strategy Team recognized the value of an inventory of the department’s data collections as a logical step to coordinate efforts. A working group was established for the inventory, and all program offices were invited to name a representative to the working group. That group now has about 20 core members. The Working Group started by identifying relevant information fields and decided to draw upon a subset of the information included in the Information Collection Request packages that are prepared for clearance by the Office of Management and Budget (OMB).
